# 💼 Fermi Estimation Practice

**Goal:** Practice structured Fermi estimation questions (e.g., "How many gas stations are there in the USA?") using a clear, logical framework.

---

### 📝 The Prompt

```text
Act as a PM Interview Coach and expert in Fermi Estimation. I'm practicing for an interview and need to solve this question:
"[INSERT ESTIMATION QUESTION - e.g., 'How many windows are there in Manhattan?']"

Guide me through the solution using this structured approach:
1. **Clarify the Scope:** What assumptions are we making? (e.g., "Does Manhattan include Roosevelt Island?").
2. **The Equation:** Break down the final number into a logical formula.
3. **Assumptions & Estimates:** Provide reasonable values for each variable (e.g., "Number of buildings", "Avg. windows per floor").
4. **Calculations:** Perform the math step-by-step.
5. **Gut Check:** Does the final number sound reasonable? Compare it to a known sanity metric.

Finally, give me 3 tips on how I could have improved my reasoning or where the biggest risk of error lies in this specific estimate.
```

---

### 💡 Pro-Tip
The final number doesn't matter as much as the *logic* you use to get there. Interviewers are looking for how you handle ambiguity and whether your assumptions are grounded in reality.
